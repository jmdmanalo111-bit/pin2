Liquidation vs. Going-Concern Value

There is a huge difference between the net value after liquidation and the value of equipment within an ongoing business with a good lease, a capable operator, and a credible business plan. Liquidation forces discounts and adds costs—often leaving very little for stakeholders.

Rule of Thumb: Net liquidated value may be as little as $20,000 after moving, storage, selling costs, and the liquidator’s fee.

What is Debt Restructuring?

Debt restructuring is a negotiated adjustment between a debtor and creditors to make obligations bearable while preserving enterprise value. Terms can include reducing principal, extending amortization, or changing interest rates. The goal is to restore financial stability and keep the business operating.
