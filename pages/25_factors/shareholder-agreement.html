Shareholder Agreement

There are no math or accounting formulas that pertain to the weight of value given to shareholders' agreements. An experienced entrepreneurial valuator is well positioned to suggest if a shareholder agreement should require legal interpretation for us to use in our valuation to determine “fair market value” as required by Canada Income Tax Act Policy Paper.

Valuators with experience in managing and owning businesses are better positioned to understand the implications of shareholder agreements on a company's value. They can assess the impact of provisions such as buy-sell agreements, voting rights, and ownership restrictions.

Meanwhile, while accountants are knowledgeable about financial aspects of shareholder agreements, they might not fully understand the practical implications of these agreements on a company's operations and value. Accountants lacking practical business experience might not grasp the implications of shareholder agreements on a company's operations and value, potentially skewing the comparability between businesses.

Business brokers may be familiar with the financial aspects of shareholder agreements, they might not fully understand the practical implications of these agreements on a company's operations and value. The practical implications of shareholder agreements on a company's operations and value might not be considered in sale price data, potentially skewing the comparability between businesses.
